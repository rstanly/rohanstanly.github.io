Regression Analysis of ESG Factors and Financial Performance in Indian Companies

1. Introduction

Environment, Social and Governance (ESG) factors are of great importance in assessing the performance of a company in our current times. Investors have moved on from traditional financial Indicators and now look into the sustainability factors like Carbon Emissions, Energy Consumption, Employee well being and Various other factors in order to see how the company is performing.

This report is focused on analyzing the impact of ESG factors like Carbon Emissions, Energy Consumption and Employee Turnover rate has impact on a companies profitability ratios like Return on Assets and Return on Equity. This report also takes into consideration the location and industry of the company to check if it has any impact on the companies profitability.

The F-test (p = 0.0632) is just above the 0.05 threshold, suggesting the overall model is marginally significant.

Conclusion: ESG factors (particularly energy use and carbon emissions) have some influence on Return on Equity, though the explanatory power remains modest.

4. Conclusions

4.1 ROA Models

Across all specifications, EC, CE, and ETOR showed no significant impact on ROA.

ROA is weakly explained and seems largely independent of ESG-related metrics in this dataset.

4.2 ROE Models

Without industry controls, log_EC and log_CE showed significant effects: higher energy consumption reduced profitability, while higher carbon emissions increased it (likely a firm-size effect).

Once industries were included, EC and CE lost significance, while industry type emerged as the dominant driver of ROE.

4.3 Overall Findings

ROA: Poorly explained by the available ESG variables; industry effects matter only marginally.

ROE: Strongly driven by EC which reduces the ROE.

ESG factors like Energy Consumption, Carbon emission and Employee turnover rate plays only a small role in the companies selected in the data set.
